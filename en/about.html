About The Company

Chateau Grona Group is one of the 10th largest wine producers in Ukraine. Our specialization is creating high-quality PRIVATE LABEL wines for China market. CHATEAU GRONA GROUP originates from a winemaking colony founded in 1927 by the French fund "ICA" on the territory of Bessarabia – an area of modern Ukraine with more than a thousand years' history of winemaking. For almost 90 years of its history, just all of Ukrainian Bessarabia, the winery survived many challenges, economic successes, and downfalls. And through all these years our winery has been one of the best known and most respected wineries of Bessarabia.

In 1926 French winemakers traveled around almost all of Bessarabia looking for the best areas for viticulture. They chose a site near the town of Kaushan, which they named "the ideal plot". The Fund built a village and a winery equipped with the best winemaking facilities available at that time. 52 hectares of vineyards with European varieties were laid down around the village.

In just a few years the colony became one of the most prosperous villages in Bessarabia, and its wines were supplied all over Europe including France. In 1937 the area was annexed by the Soviet Union. After World War II the colonists never returned to the village. In 1997-2000 the Blumberg family from Odesa bought the company, which thus became one of the first private wineries in Ukraine. The winery has since been renovated and provided with brand new equipment, including a bottling line. Currently, our vineyards cover 340 hectares.

Our vineyards are located on the "grape latitude" – between 46 and 47 degrees –the same as the famous French wine regions of Bordeaux and Burgundy. The combination of black soil and sandy soil with hilly terrain provides the best natural conditions for wine. The climate of Ukrainian Bessarabia naturally combines long, sunny summers and short, relatively mild winters with frequent snowfall. This allows us to get abundant harvests of high quality. We grow European varieties which are the most suitable for the area: Cabernet Sauvignon, Merlot, Bastardo, Chardonnay, Sauvignon Blanc, Muscats (Irshai Oliver, Traminer, etc.), as well as local and rare varieties such as Odessa Black, Saperavi, Sukholimansky, Telti-Kuruk.

The Company Today

In the years 2011-2016 the company made the main emphasis on the development of vineyards, production and bulk sale of wine (the B2B sector), as well as bottling limited quantities of wine for the HoReCa segment. We developed a strong reputation by supplying bulk wine to the largest Ukrainian wineries, as well as exporting to Russia, Belarus, China, Vietnam, and Spain. Since 2016 the company also expanded its presence on the Ukrainian consumer market in the fast-growing segment of wine in a bag-in-box package where we are now clearly No.1 in Ukraine.

At the same time, we started to develop the new way – private label production – by using our competitive advantages: own vineyards, a very strong team of winemakers and salesmen, and high competence in flexible production and export.

We produce wines under customer's private labels since 2016, making an accent on two main target markets: Asian (especially Chinese) and domestic. Having more than ten regular contracts CHATEAU GRONA GROUP earned a high reputation between customers as a reliable and flexible supplier of high-quality wines.

In 2018 the company acquired an additional line for the bag-in-box wines production and expanded the production license for fruit-flavored wines, as well as low-alcohol wine drinks. At the end of 2018 Chateau Grona is among the top 10 Ukrainian wineries for the production of wine for consumer market and #1 in bag-in-box wine production in Ukraine In years 2018-2019 company develops private label and bulk wine sales in China, Vietnam, and Kazakhstan.

The Team

Despite its active development "Chateau Grona" remains a privately owned company and preserves a "family" style of management. The owners are at the same time top-managers who deal with finance, supply chain, and marketing. Most of the production managers, including the winemaker and the chief agronomist, are inhabitants of Lambrovka and the neighboring villages.

"Chateau Grona" is a socially responsible company that cares about its employees and their families. As a result, the staff perceives the company as its own, investing not only time and energy but also soul into the cultivation of grapes and the creation of fine wines.
