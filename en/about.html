About Us

The “AGRO-YUG” winery (a member of CHATEAU GRONA GROUP) originates from a winemaking colony founded in 1927 by the French fund “ICA” on the territory of Bessarabia – an area of modern Ukraine with more than a thousand years’ history of winemaking. For almost 90 years of its history, just all of Ukrainian Bessarabia, the winery survived many challenges, economic successes and downfalls. And through all these years our winery has been one of the best known and most respected wineries of Bessarabia.

In 1926 French winemakers travelled around almost all of Bessarabia looking for the best areas for viticulture. They chose a site near the town of Kaushan, which they named “the ideal plot”. The Fund built a village and a winery equipped with the best winemaking facilities available at that time. 52 hectares of vineyards with European varieties were laid down around the village. In just a few years the colony became one of the most prosperous villages in Bessarabia, and its wines were supplied all over Europe including France. In 1937 the area was annexed by the Soviet Union. After World War II the colonists never returned to the village.
In 1997-2000 the Bliumberg family from Odessa bought the company, which thus became one of the first private wineries in Ukraine. The winery has since been renovated and provided with brand new equipment, including a bottling line. Currently our vineyards cover 340 hectares.
Our vineyards are located on the “grape latitude” – between 46 and 47 degrees –the same as the famous French wine regions of Bordeaux and Burgundy. The combination of black soil and sandy soil with hilly terrain provides the best natural conditions for wine. The climate of Ukrainian Bessarabia naturally combines long, sunny summers and short, relatively mild winters with frequent snowfall. This allows us to get abundant harvests of high quality.
We grow European varieties which are the most suitable for the area: Cabernet Sauvignon, Merlot, Bastardo, Chardonnay, Sauvignon Blanc, Muscats (Irshai Oliver, Traminer, etc.), as well as local and rare varieties such as Odessa Black, Saperavi, Sukholimansky, Telti-Kuruk.

History

In the twenties of the last century ICA, aFund owned by Baron de Hirsch (Paris, France),created a Jewish agricultural commune on the territory of the then Kingdom of Romania. The commune became the prototype of the modern Israeli “moshav”.

In 1926 French agronomists travelled around almost all of Bessarabia looking for the best areas for viticulture. They chose a site near the town of Kaushan, which they named “the ideal plot”. The land (about 60 acres) has been reclaimed and in 1927 the Lambrovka village was founded practically as a green field site. The Fund built 52 houses, a winery, and a dairy farm. Around the village, 52 hectares of vineyards were laid down. Then the Fund moved 52 Jewish families from neigbouring villages and towns in Bessarabia to Lambrovka . Each family received 1 hectare of vineyard as their own property, as well as 6 cows and sheep. French agronomists and winemakers were managing the project. The winery was provided with the latest equipment available at that time.
In just a few years Lambrovka became one of most prosperous villages in Bessarabia and its wines were supplied all over Europe including France.
In 1937 the area was annexed by the Soviet Union, but the Jewish community continued to exist until the beginning of World War II. The entire population of Lambrovka was evacuated to Central Asia, and after the war the colonists did not return to the village, moving mostly to the U.S. and Israel.
From the beginning of the 50s the winery was restored. Throughout the following 40 years, “Grona” (the new name of winery) has been the leading winemaking enterprise of Tarutino district, which in turn is one of the largest grape zones in Ukraine and the territory of the former Soviet Union.
After the collapse of the Soviet Union, “Grona” found itself in a very difficult situation, and the production of wine was almost stopped.
In 1997-2000 Bliumberg family from Odessa bought a controlling stake in the company, which has thus become one of the first private wineries in Ukraine. Since then the third stage of Lambrovka history has begun. The winery has been restored and provided with brand new equipment, including a bottling line. Wines from Lambrovka produced by “AGRO-YUG, LLC” became popular throughout the independent Ukraine, and were also being supplied to Germany.
In 2002 a delegation of the ICA fund headed by its President, Baronet Stephen Waley-Cohen visited Lambrovka. Representatives of the UK, France and Israel inspected the new vineyards, tasted wines and participated in Lambrovka’s 75th anniversary celebrations.

The Company Today

Today the main emphasis is made on the development of vineyards, production and bulk sale of wine (the B2B sector), as well as bottling limited quantities of wine for the HoReCa segment.
In 2011-14 the winery has developed a strong reputation among Ukrainian wine producers, and signed a number of big contracts.
Among our partners are the largest wineries in Ukraine, including the “Crimean Wine House” (TM Oreanda), “Inkerman Winery” (TM Inkerman), “Atlantis” (Golden Amphora TM), the Odessa factory of sparkling wines, and many others.
In 2015 the company expanded its presence in the Ukrainian market and other export markets.

The Team

Despite its active development “Chateau Grona” remains a privately owned companyand preserves a “family” style of management. The owners are at the same time top-managers who deal with finance, supply chain and marketing. Most of the production managers, including the winemaker and the chief agronomist are inhabitants of Lambrovka and the neighbouring villages.
“Chateau Grona” is a socially responsible company that cares about its employees and their families. As a result the staff perceives the company as its own, investing not only time and energy, but also soul into the cultivation of grapes and creation of fine wines.
